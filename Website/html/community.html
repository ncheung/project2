Mumford and Sons Concert

Over the weekend, Mumford and Sons had an amazing concert at Holland Park in Surrey. Some 25,000 people gathered together to enjoy an unbelievable live performance that was out of this world. The outdoor show was a huge success and left everyone wanting more.

The Grammy-winning band composed of Marcus Mumford, Ben Lovett, Winston Marshall and Ted Dwane began promptly at 8 p.m., to the overwhelmingly deafening sound of applause. Visibly taken aback by the magnitude of support given by the audience, Mumford thanked the crowd and proceeded to lead the band through a crazy rendition of Little Lion Man as thousands of people clapped along.

For almost two hours, Mumford and Sons transformed Holland Park into their own public stomping ground as thousands of people continued to cheer them on throughout the night. Mumford and Sons kept the crowd entranced by the power of their music, through the slower sets (Ghost That We Knew, Where Are You Now?) all the way to the encore performance of The Cave.

Unsurprisingly, Mumford and Sons have become a huge name within the indie rock genre. Their amazing performance at Holland Park has definitely set high standards for their summer tour.

- Jamie Duggan
